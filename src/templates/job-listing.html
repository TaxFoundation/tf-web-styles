Duties and Responsibilities

  • Provide general support to the Vice President of Individual and Foundation Giving.
  • Engage in identifying, cultivating and soliciting gifts from donors identified with a propensity and capacity to make four and five-figure gifts.
  • Develop strategies for upgrading donors and increasing donor retention.
  • Engage in donor research in order to identify high net worth donors and prospects. Record all information in donor profile.
  • Generate and distribute personal donor letters, handwritten notes, and emails on a regular basis in order to thank donors for gifts and arrange for personal visits.
  • Manage the various donor recognition programs to ensure the programs are sound, are being promoted, and donor benefits and inquiries are being fulfilled.
  • Work proactively and collaboratively with all Tax Foundation staff members and assist in scheduling visits with donors for staff while they are traveling.
  • Generate Salesforce reports on an as-needed bases to support various development needs.
  • Participate in regular donor strategy discussions and meetings.
  • Assist with planning of donor-centered events including.
  • Document all contact with donors and prospects to ensure positive and purposeful donor relations.
  • erform other duties as assigned. This position reports to the VP of Individual and Foundation Giving.

Qualifications and Requirements

  • Passionate about the Tax Foundation, tax policy and economic growth.
  • Bachelor’s Degree and a minimum of 3 years’ experience in fundraising or public policy, ideally in non-partisan, policy oriented, 501(c)(3) organizations.
  • Understand and embrace a “moves-management” approach to major gift fundraising. Demonstrated record of exceeding goals.
  • Working knowledge of Salesforce or similar CRM database.
  • Organized, self-motivated, creative, and able to work independently.
  • Excellent interpersonal and communication skills, both verbal and written.
  • Must be comfortable making cold calls and hand-writing letters.
  • Adhere to the highest ethical standards, demonstrate empathetic disposition, and perseverance.
  • Project an optimistic and positive attitude and convey sensitivity to needs of the donors.
  • Able and willing to travel.
  • Have a valid driver’s license and able to drive long distances for several hours at a time.
  • This position is based in Washington DC.

Benefits

Salary is commensurate with experience. Benefits include HSA-based health coverage (TF provides two-thirds of the premium and deposits the amount of the deductible into the HSA), dental and vision coverage, life/AD&D/LTD insurance, 12 holidays per year, 15 vacation days per year, sick leave, early release on Fridays, and a generous 401(k) retirement plan (100% match on first 3%; 50% match on next 2%).

Applying

Submit cover letter and resume to Mr. Joseph Henchman, VP Operations, Tax Foundation, at henchman@taxfoundation.org. The cover letter should demonstrate informed interest in our organization. The position is open until filled. The Tax Foundation is an equal opportunity employer. EOE/M/F/D/V
